The 2024 International AI Applications in Public Health and Social Services (AI-PHSS 2024)

 

The AIPH workshop explores how AI technologies enhance public health and social services, focusing on revolutionizing surveillance, tackling health issues, and informing policy decisions. It highlights AI's potential to amplify community engagement, streamline social worker case management, and optimize service delivery. The workshop will bring together researchers, practitioners, healthcare professionals, and policymakers to share insights, exchange ideas, and foster collaborations in these critical areas. We invite submissions of papers and posters that investigate AI applications in public health and social services, such as predictive modeling for chronic disease management, real-world data analysis, sentiment analysis in social services, resource allocation optimization, and AI-driven decision support systems in public health and social services. Through interdisciplinary exchange, we aim to advance understanding of AI's role in addressing public health challenges and improving social service provision efficiency.

We are inviting original research submissions as well as work-in-progress to the Workshop on AI Applications in Public Health and Social Services (AI-PHSS).

§  Topics:

Topics of interest include but are not limited to:

o   Predictive modeling for disease surveillance and outbreak detection.

o   Sentiment analysis and natural language processing for community health engagement

o   AI-driven decision support systems for social workers in case management.

o   Optimization algorithms for resource allocation in social service delivery.

o   Machine learning approaches for early detection and intervention in public health issues.

o   Data mining techniques for identifying patterns and trends in public health data.

o   Ethical considerations in deploying AI technologies in public health and social services.

o   Human-AI collaboration frameworks for enhancing public health and social services.

§  Motivation:

The intersection of AI and public health/social services (PHSS) holds significant relevance for AIME participants, given its potential to address pressing societal challenges. With the proliferation of PHSS data and advancements in AI technologies, there's a growing interest in leveraging these tools to enhance public health surveillance, refine healthcare delivery, and streamline social service provision. This resonates with AIME's overarching theme of applying AI in medicine. Given the urgency of issues like the COVID-19 pandemic and the burden of chronic diseases, exploring AI's role in shaping more effective interventions and policy decisions is crucial for the AIME community.

The workshop anticipates attracting top-tier, unpublished contributions from leading researchers, practitioners, and policymakers in the PHSS field. We aim to invite esteemed scientists and experts who using AI in PHSS to submit papers and posters. This includes researchers from renowned institutions deeply engaged in AI-driven healthcare innovations, such as those at Johns Hopkins University, Harvard University, University of Washington, University of Michigan, Columbia University, Yale University, Emory University, University of Oxford, University College London, University of Toronto, Heidelberg University, University of Munich, University of Bordeaux, University of Bologna, Peking University, Sun Yat-sen University, and Tshinghua University. We will also extend invitations to participants from prominent industrial research labs, such as Google Health, Microsoft Healthcare, IBM Watson Health, and Amazon Web Services (AWS). These organizations are at the forefront of AI-driven innovations in healthcare and social services, actively developing groundbreaking solutions to address complex challenges.  Their participation ensures a diverse array of cutting-edge research findings and practical insights, fostering robust discussions and knowledge exchange among attendees.

§  Former workshops: Not applicable

§  Format

We are calling for full papers (regular and short papers) and abstracts on novel approaches, work in progress, original state-of-the-art projects in utilizing AI in PHSS, tools and resources for AI in PHSS, and cases studies. AI-PHSS 2024 will be a half-day workshop that consists of a keynote speech, an invited talk, podium talks for accepted papers, and poster presentations.

§  Intended length: Half-day.

§  Audience: Who and how many people are likely to attend?

The audience will encompass a diverse range of professionals and stakeholders interested in the intersection of AI with public health and social services (PHSS), including researchers, undergraduates, graduate students, healthcare professionals (such as physicians, nurses, public health officials, and epidemiologists), industry professionals in health AI, as well as social workers and professionals, policymakers and government officials. They have a keen interest in leveraging AI technologies to address public health challenges, improve healthcare delivery, and enhance social service provision.

Huanmei Wu, PhD, Temple University College of Public Health, huanemi.wu@temple.edu

Dr. Wu is a professor and the Chair of the Department of Health Services Administration and Policy as well as the Assistant Dean for Global Engagement at Temple University’s College of Public Health. Dr. Wu’s research focuses on translational and transformative public health, social work, and biomedical informatics research on scientific database design and utilization, big data analytics, health informatics, bioinformatics, and precision medicine. She has led projects that explore the roles and applications of data management, AI/ML, knowledge discovery, and predictive modeling to life science and public health research. Dr. Wu also organized and chaired several symposiums and workshops, including the one-day workshop on Health Informatics Education at ICHI 2022, 2023, and 2024 and another one-day workshop on translating scientific discoveries into action at ICIBM 2022.

Evangelia Katsoulakis, MD, University of South Florida (USF), ekatsoulakis@usf.edu

Dr. Katsoulakis, a board-certified radiation oncologist, channels her passion for patient care through data science and health informatics. Currently, she directs Head and Neck Services at Tampa General Hospital and the USF Health Morsani College of Medicine while also serving as Director of Radiation Oncology Clinical Informatics and Associate Professor at the University of South Florida. Dr. Katsoulakis collaborates with Moffitt Cancer Center to explore big data applications in radiomics and genomics. A founding member of the digital twin in healthcare consortium, she previously led the Radiosurgery Program at New York-Presbyterian (Cornell-Columbia) Brooklyn Methodist Hospital, earning induction into its Healers' Hall of Fame. Additionally, she is a prolific scientific author and an accomplished artist, with her oil paintings adorning the covers of the International Journal of Radiation Oncology.

Hongfang Liu, PhD, University of Texas Health Science Center at Houston (UTHealth Houston), Hongfang.liu@uth.tmc.edu

Dr. Liu currently holds the position of Professor at McWilliams School of Biomedical Informatics and is Vice President of Learning Health System at UTHealth Houston. Leading research at the Center for Translational AI Excellence and Applications in Medicine (TEAM-AI), Dr. Liu previously served as the Dr. Richard F. Emslander Professor of Biomedical Informatics at Mayo Clinic, directing the Biomedical Informatics program at Center for Clinical and Translational Sciences (CCaTS) and leading Cancer Data Science and Informatics at Mayo Clinic Comprehensive Cancer Center (MCCC) before joining McWilliams in 2023. Dr. Liu aims to advance healthcare AI by advocating the "RITE-FAIR-TIES" principle, emphasizing scientific rigor and ethical transparency to improve health outcomes for all.
